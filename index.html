10 Common Causes of Foodborne Illness — and How to Avoid Them

1

Improper Cooling

  • Food must be cooled from 60°C to 20°C in 2 hours or less, and then from 20°C to 4°C in 4 hours or less.

  • To ensure proper cooling, divide large food items (e.g., pots of soup, large cuts of meat) into smaller pieces.

  • Do not tightly cover cooling food. This prevents heat from escaping.

2

Advance Preperation

  • Prepare foods as close to serving time as possible.

  • If foods must be prepared in advance, ensure they are properly cooled and refridgerated.

  • PHFs should be thrown out if unused within 3 days of preparation.

3

Infected Person

  • Food handlers with signs of infection should be sent home.

  • Ensure food handlers wash their hands properly after any activity that could contaminate their hands.

  • PHFs should be thrown out if unused within 3 days of preparation.

4

Inadequate Reheating for Hot Holding

  • Reheat food rapidly to 74°C (hold the temperature there for at least 15 seconds) before placing in hot holding unit

  • Ensure rapid heating takes under 2 hours.

  • If reheating with a microwave, ensure the food is rotated or stirred at least once during the reheating process, as microwaves do not cook evenly.

5

Improper Hot Holding

  • Ensure hot holding units are functioning properly

  • Do not attempt to cool food by turning off the heat on holding units. Food in hot holding units should be properly cooled as soom as meal time has ended.

  • Preheat hot hold units to at least 60°C before placing hot food in it.

6

Contaminated Raw Food or Ingredient

  • Look, taste, and smell are not reliable ways to check for pathogens in food.

  • Buy all foods from approved suppliers.

  • Never serve raw food to high-risk customers.

7

Unsafe Source

  • Foods from approved sources have a much lower chance of causing illness.

  • To check if a source is approved, contact your local environmental health officer.

  • Do not take chances when it comes to food safety. One outbreak could ruin your company's reputation.

8

Use of Leftovers

  • Only reheat leftovers once. Do not attempt to reuse reheated leftovers.

  • Follow proper cooling and reheating procedures.

  • Never mix leftovers and fresh foods.

9

Cross-Contamination

  • Use seperate cooking equipment for raw and ready-to-eat foods.

  • Always wash hands between tasks.

  • Store raw and ready-to-eat foods in different areas.

10

Inadequate Cooking

  • Always use a thermometer to determine whether food is cooked.

  • Check the temperature in multiple spots on larfge cuts of meat / large batches of food.

  • When frying or grilling raw meat, cook until the juices run clear.
